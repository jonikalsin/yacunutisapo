Who Is Cheer's Jeron Hazelwood? His Bio, Age, Partner, Height

August 2024 · 5 minute read

Everything has changed in Jeron (Lovin) Hazelwood’s life since the day he landed on Cheer Season 2.

Cheer returned on Netflix with a second season on 12 January 2022 with some Season 1 favorites and with some newest competitors ready to steal the Daytona crown. Jeron Hazelwood is among the newcomers and from Navarro’s rivals Trinity Valley Community College.

And now, we shall tell you all about him in this ‘Jeron Hazelwood Bio’.

Jeron Hazelwood On Netflix’s Cheer Season 2

Cheer Season 2 sees several new cast members Navarro’s latest recruit — Maddy Brum, the child star Cassadee Dunlap, and Rookie Gillion Rupert — and a few of the team coaches in Navarro’s rivals Trinity Valley Community College that include Khris Franklin, tumblers DeVonte Dee Joseph, Angel Rice, Jada Wooten, and Jeron Hazelwood (of course).

Jeron, dubbed as Trinity Valley Community College’s star stunter and tumbler, is the leader of the group alongside its other leader Jada Wooten.

On the show, you shall see for yourself, how his technical skills, positive attitude, and standout performances prove to be key to his team.

Before becoming a part of Cheer Season 2, Jeron has cheered for Gymtyme Chrome and Cheer Athletics.

As you know, Jeron studied for his associate’s degree in Business Administration and Management at Trinity Valley Community College from 2017 – 2019.

To know more about him Jeron has requested you all to tune in to Netflix early in January 2022 for his exceptional story. “I can’t wait for everyone to be able to share this experience, it’s one you don’t want to miss”, he said of his Netflix debut.

Jeron Hazelwood Age

Jeron Hazelwood turned 26 years old on 15 October 2021. And on this day, he took to his social media to thank everyone who reach out to him. Then he wrote: “It’s truly a blessing being able to walk this earth for 26 years. I’ve seen, experienced, learned, grown, failed, lost, and gained a lot within this last 25th rotation around the world. I’m praying, wishing, manifesting, and hoping that the 26th is even better. Happy birthday to me, and all the Libras out there in the world! IT’S A LIBRA PARTY!”

Does Jeron Hazelwood Have A Partner?

Jeron Hazelwood’s relationship status on Facebook is marked as ‘single’.

Also, Jeron is openly gay but has not revealed anything yet about his dating life be it in the past or in recent times.

Jeron Hazelwood Height

Reportedly, Jeron Hazelwood stands 6 feet tall.

Jeron Hazelwood Family

It’s his mother Karen Hazelwood who means the world to Jeron Hazelwood.

The matriarch features many a time on her son’s social media and just as Jeron claims himself to be the twin of his mother, they indeed look so similar.

Jeron also says that his mother has always been his biggest support in life. “My mom is my world and I love her so much!! :).”

Likewise, on Mother’s Day recently, Jeron also included his sister in his Mother’s Day message. Sharing a picture of him, his sister, and his mother, he captioned — “Happy Mother’s Day to these two very important ladies in my life. It’s great to be raised by both of you, and I love you both very very dearly. Funny to say that these two are not only my backbones, but they are my best friends. Love you, mom and sis.”

Then, he also worded “We a cute family” in the hashtag.

Jeron’s seemingly only sister is on Instagram @itsme_bishes23.

  • What Does Jeron Hazelwood Do For A Living?

As per LinkedIn, Jeron Hazelwood is a Cheer coach at Gymtyme Allstars in Atlanta Metropolitan Area; that, he has been a coach there until the time of this writing as of August 2015.

On his LinkedIn, Jeron also went on explaining that he has been pursuing Sports for several years now and that he has coached basketball since the age of 16.

Jeron says he loves being on the court and helping players make better decisions. “Skills that I can bring to the table are hard-working, competitive, and very determined.” In addition, he shares that his future goals consist of playing college basketball at the Division 1 level. Even so, he is ready to get his head down and do whatever it takes to achieve the goals. Then, after college, Jeron would like to be a sports agent or coach and help young players become great agents.

Meanwhile, on Facebook, Jeron had been constantly talking about his AT&T job. A couple of times, from around December 2020 – January 2021, we caught him persuading his people on Facebook to save some money and buy high-speed cable and internet from him at a reasonable price.
