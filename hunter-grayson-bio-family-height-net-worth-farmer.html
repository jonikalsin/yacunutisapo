What does a farmer do when he wants a wife? He brings eight women to his homestead and chooses one of them to his liking! But don’t take our word for it. Watch Hunter Grayson do so in Fox’s new dating show Farmer Wants a Wife.

This Hunter Grayson Bio explores his life.

Hunter Grayson On Farmer Wants A Wife

It’s “nonsense” (as Ew.com labeled it) but interesting. Fox’s Farmer Wants a Wife is a reality dating show hosted by country star Jennifer Nettles. Here, 32 hopelessly single city women move to the countryside in hopes of finding love with one of the four farmers — Hunter Grayson, Ryan Black, Landon Heaton, and Allen Foster.

Each farmer will take eight women to his homestead for a chance at romance whilst struggling with the hassles of daily farm life —  livestock, hay, and hard manual labor.

A short-lived U.S. version of Farmer Wants a Wife also aired on the CW back in 2008. So, a bold move to bring the show back after 15 years certainly means that Farmer Wants a Wife has stepped up its game.

Now, talking about the single women competing to be Hunter’s wife, they come from different cities and work in a wide array of jobs. Namely, they are DeVonne (Director of marketing, from Scottsdale, AZ), Emma (Dance coach, from Pacific Palisades, CA), Hayley (HR manager, from Chicago, IL), Meghan (Event coordinator, from Midland, TX), Sarah R. (Content creator, from Kansas City, MO), Stephanie (Sommelier, from Temecula, CA), Sydney (Music booking agent, from Nashville, TN), and Talia (Therapist, from Atlanta, GA),.

Hunter Grayson Relationship Status

Hunter Grayson was always private about his dating life, and now even more as he signed the NDA with Farmer Wants a Wife. But though the star hesitated to reveal who he might have wifed up on the show, he was open about the type of partner he was looking for.

“I don’t need a business partner. I’m not looking for a business partner. Neither a ranch hand. I’m looking for my partner in life,” he said. “I don’t care where you come from, I don’t care where you’ve been. It’s where we’re gonna go that’s gonna make a difference to me.”

Hearing this, his co-star, Allen Foster also nodded in agreement and added, “It’s what she’s made of, not how she was raised. It’s what she’s made of.”

How Old Is Hunter Grayson?

Hunter Grayson was 31 years of age when he appeared on Farmer Wants a Wife in 2023.

However, he was a year younger when he filmed the show. This also means most of his possible wives were younger than him with the youngest at 22, Sydney.

Hunter Grayson Family

Hunter Grayson comes from a family of four — his parents, Cezanne “Cissy” Potts Wood and Ricky Wood, himself, and his sister

Sadly, his mother, Cissy took her last breath on May 27, 2019, after a struggle with some health issues. “Ole Cissy was something else, she could light up a room, make you laugh, cuss, cry, and is probably the reason I have some grey in my beard…. but nonetheless she was my mama and I will truly miss her,” Hunter paid a tribute to her on his IG.

Also, a year after her death, Hunter recalled his mother and shared she was the woman who” taught me my love for singing, made me so “particular” about how my hair looks, cooked all my favorite meals, drove me insane, and always came home surci’s (momma’s surprise gifts).”

Cezanne Potts was born in Athens, Georgia, and she spent her life as an educator at Johnson High School, West Hall High School, Douglas County Schools, Oconee County Schools, and Stephens County School System.

As for Hunter’s father Ricky J Wood, he turned 67 in March 2023 and worked at Georgia Transport Company at the time. The star said that he was the best dad in the world.

Now, talking about Hunter’s sister Jessica Grayson Skelton, she’s married with a kid. “Even if she fusses at me about diabetes, or tattle tails on me at 5 am about a tattoo I didn’t even get! she’s a pretty cool sister to put up with me,” he summarized her.

Also, Hunter has two step-siblings named Brittany Ann, and Ricky Kyle Wood. They were 35 and 39 years of age respectively in 2023.

Hunter Grayson Job

Hunter Grayson is a singer/songwriter and course a farmer.

This farm boy started his music journey playing various instruments early on in his childhood. However, it wasn’t until the summer of 2015 that he decided to play guitar more seriously, and the next year, he formed a group “Hunter Grayson & The Hat Creek Band” to play with him. Soon, the band would go took book houses full shows and performs across the south-southeast. 

Now, Hunter’s originals as Tipsy, Man I’ll Never Be, Drinking Drive Her, and more are available on Spotify as well.

Trivia: Hunter received the prestigious award of the “Overall & Male Artists of the Year” in 2018 at the Georgia County Awards.
