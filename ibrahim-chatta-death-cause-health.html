Ibrahim Chatta | Death Cause | Health

April 2024 · 4 minute read

The news of Ibrahim Chatta’s death has been trending over the internet. The actor is still alive and healthy, thus the information is untrue.

Ibrahim Chatta, a talented actor from Nigeria, is also a writer, producer, and singer. He has acted in and created more than 100 soundtracks for Yoruba movies.

He is a well-known Nollywood performer who has won the City People Movie Award for Best Actor of the Year (Yoruba).

What happened to Nigerian actor Ibrahim Chatta after the death hoax? Health Report

The news of Ibrahim Chatta’s passing spread after he uploaded a photo on his Instagram account. He was shown in the photo laying on a hospital bed with pipes and cables all around him. The caption said, “Pls Stay Safe.”

He was being given blood and oxygen in the image. In response, some said the Nollywood actor was ill while others said he had passed away.

Ibrahim Chatta answered, dispelling the claims, claiming he was still alive. To keep his followers informed of his most recent endeavors, he said that the shot was taken during the production of a movie scene.

Ibrahim Chatta also thanked the individuals who phoned to inquire about his health and expressed his regret to his fans and supporters for the trouble this had created.

Ibrahim Chatta is therefore healthy and living as of right now. The 52-year-old is now developing his forthcoming movies.

Ibrahim Chatta, Who Is He? Bio of Wikipedia

Ibrahim Chatta, whose real name is Ibrahim Abiodun Chatta, is a seasoned Nollywood performer, writer, producer, and singer. On October 13, 1970, he was born in Bachita, Kwara State, a Nigerian middle belt state.

While his mother is from Modakeke, Osun State, the actor is of Tapa (Nupe) ancestry. He hails from a huge, 14-child Muslim household.

Ibrahim received his education at Bachita’s Saint Bridges Catholic Primary School, where he graduated. Then, due to his behavior, he transferred to Aggiea, Niger State, from a government secondary school.

Ibrahim Chatta had a reputation for being rowdy in his younger years, frequently getting into fights and causing trouble. After enrolling, he left Dende Secondary School in Aggiea to pursue his education through online courses and programs.

Details of Ibrahim Chatta’s Career

Ibrahim always had a passion for the theater and would go to performances whenever a troupe came to town. He saw performances by great actors like the late Duro Ladipo, Adebayo Salami, and Ray Eyiwunmi (Oga Bello).

Ibrahim was motivated to pursue acting by these actions. In 1985, he had a significant launch for his professional career with Joseph Laoye (Uncle Joe), a Baba Ray product.

Ibrahim had a strong command of Yoruba despite being of Tapa descent, and his proficiency in the language paved the door for his prosperous career as a Yoruba actor.

His well-known films include Aiyekoto, Affi Wonmi, Dudu, Akisa, Eni, IrawoOke, and Aiye Dudu.

Additionally, Ibrahim is a vocalist. He has created several Yoruba advertising jingles and more than 100 soundtracks.

Along with releasing hip-hop songs like Area, My Money, Friday Night, and Boom Boom Boom, he has also worked with Alhaji Abdulkabir Alayande (Ere Asalatu).
