Professional Life

Professionally, Daryl Sabara is an American actor. He started acting during the mid-1990s, appearing on episodes of Murphy Brown, Life’s Work, and Will and Grace. Later, he was cast as Juni Cortez in the Spy Kids series of family films, which became popular among pre-teen audiences.

Likewise, he provided the voice of Hunter in the Father of Pride. He also made appearances in several episodes of the television series Weeds, House, and Dr. Vegas. He also became one of the judges on America’s Most Talented Kids. He voiced the main character, Hero Boy, in the 2004 animated version of The Polar Express.

Then, he played the role of a Jewish young boy trying to have a nice Bar Mitzvah in Keeping Up with the Steins (formerly known as Lucky 13). It releases on May 12, 2006. He made a guest appearance in the Criminal Minds episode, “P911” where he played a sexually molested child. He even provided the voice for Rex in Generator Rex.

Next, he acted in the films Her Best Movie and the interactive DVD Choose Your Adventure, played Ben on The Last Chance Detectives radio dramas by Adventures in Odyssey, and Focus on the Family. He also appeared as school bully Wesley Rhoades in Rob Zombie’s Halloween. Then, he played Irwin in the theater play The Catskills Sonata, about a young Jewish busboy in the late 1950s.
