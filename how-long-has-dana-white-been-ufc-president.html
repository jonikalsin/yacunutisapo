How long has Dana White been UFC president?

May 2024 Â· 2 minute read

Dana White has been a prominent figure in the UFC since 2001, and his journey from a boxing gym owner to the president of the world's premier MMA organization is remarkable. Let's delve into the significant milestones of White's tenure as the UFC president.

Taking the Helm: 2001-2023

  • In 2001, Dana White became the president of the UFC after his childhood friends, Lorenzo and Frank Fertitta, acquired the company. This marked the beginning of White's influential leadership role.
  • White's outspoken nature and business acumen contributed to the UFC's tremendous growth under his leadership. By 2015, the UFC's gross revenue had reached $600 million.
  • Despite controversies and challenges, White's impact on the sport of MMA is undeniable, as he continued to lead the UFC through its surge in popularity in 2005 and beyond.

    • Noteworthy Events During White's Tenure

  • In 2020, Dana White publicly campaigned for former POTUS Donald Trump during the US presidential elections, showcasing his close relationship with the administration.
  • White's relationship with incumbent US president Donald Trump has sparked controversy due to the UFC's continued support of the president, despite Trump's controversial actions and statements.
  • White's public disputes with notable fighters, his defense of the UFC's pay scale, and his involvement in disputes over fighter pay and contract issues have made headlines during his tenure.

    • Transition to UFC CEO: 2023

  • In 2023, White was promoted to the role of UFC CEO, marking a significant transition from his longstanding position as the UFC president.
  • White's vision for the future of the UFC includes expanding its global reach and continuing to grow the sport of MMA.

    • Future Prospects

  • In 2023, White signed a new seven-year contract with the UFC's parent company, Endeavor, indicating his intentions to stay on as president of the MMA organization for the foreseeable future.
  • The extended distribution deal with ESPN solidifies the UFC's position in the sports industry, with White expressing excitement for the upcoming ventures.

    • Dana White's tenure as the UFC president has been marked by significant milestones, controversies, and his unwavering influence on the sport of MMA. As he continues to lead the UFC in the capacity of CEO, his impact on the organization and the sport at large remains a topic of great interest and debate.
