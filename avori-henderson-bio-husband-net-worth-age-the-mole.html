Avori Henderson On Netflix’s The Mole

Netflix’s newest offering “The Mole” the streaming platform shares that the whole show is based on lies. The show is the reboot of ABC’s series based on the same name which ran for five seasons previously. The ten-episode show spreads across three weeks and sees 12 contestants competing together in challenges to add money to a pot that only one of them will win at the end.

But one player has been designated the role of “mole” by producers to sabotage the rest of the players from their money-making efforts. Depending on how well the mole performs their task, which harms the tasks and reduces the prize money, the participants’ earnings are determined. One player will ultimately outlast the others and uncover the Mole to take home the cash.

Avori Henderson is one of the contestants. Being a professional gamer who competed against 55 men on Team USA, she believes she has the upper hand in this competition. Her strategy going in is to “watch every detail of every player and spot inconsistencies.” She has a background in problem-solving however she believes in people’s kind words which is her weakness.

How Much Is Avori Henderson Net Worth?

Avori Henderson’s net worth should be above $2 million.

Avori is a former beauty pageant model turned gamer, content creator, and philanthropist. She walked from a promising pageantry career and started gaming to create original and unique content. She started streaming full-time on Facebook and garnered a huge fanbase and hosted the first-ever all-female PUBG tournament in 2019. The stream had 135,806 unique viewers, 166,342 live views, and 7,235 concurrent viewers.

The event raised over $10,000 to benefit various charities including FredHutch and the Children’s Miracle Network. Being the sole female participant in the competition, she has traversed the world since the commencement of the event to promote women in e-sports and compete in tournaments in Sweden, France, San Diego, Los Angeles, Houston, San Antonio, Boston, and Mexico City.

Avori told Esports Heaven that she dropped out of Miss America Pageant to pursue gaming. She added, “I was in the heat of competing as I just won my local title and I  had prepped all year to take home the state title. A week before the competition, I got a call from my State Director asking for all of my information regarding the pageant.”

Her realization regarding the politics involved surrounding Miss America made her drop out from the Miss America competition and pursued the online tournament the same week during the pageant. She started pageantry by competing in the Miss Missouri pageant in Mexico, Missouri in 2016.

Growing up, Avori had made her goal to model professionally and was also signed by Ford Models and Hollister. When her father turned fatally ill when she was 16, it was hard for her and her family. So, she decided to do something with her life thus she signed up for her first pageant where she could advocate for her dad’s health situation and try to educate others on how to avoid such illnesses.

Avori became the 2013 Sweet Corn Pageant Runner-up and 2015 Miss Missouri Teen USA 16th place out of 65.

Avori was once banned from Twitch, without any explanation. She believes it was because she was wearing a tank top underneath after she removed a jacket and her body art on her shoulder. Her page was down for 24 hours.

In addition to gaming, she has her own clothing line named Styles By Avori. You can also find her on iTunes, Spotify, Pandora, and Apple Music.

Avori completed her high school at Glenwood High School and attended Lindenwood University where she was a cheerleader for the 2014-15 Lionettes Roster.
